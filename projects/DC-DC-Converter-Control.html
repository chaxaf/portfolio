Analysis and Control of SAB and PSFB Converters

Semester project at EPFL’s Power Electronics Laboratory where I designed and tuned control strategies for high-power Single Active Bridge (SAB) and Phase-Shifted Full Bridge (PSFB) DC/DC converters intended for medium-voltage DC grids fed by renewable sources. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

Overview

Large-scale integration of renewables requires isolated, efficient DC/DC conversion between a 5 kV input and a 20 kV DC collection grid at tens of megawatts. I studied two promising topologies – SAB and PSFB – and focused on how well they can keep the input DC link voltage stable when the source and grid introduce disturbances. The work was entirely simulation-based (PLECS/Matlab) and compared both steady-state and dynamic behaviour under the same operating conditions.

Objectives

  • Develop a simple PI-based input DC link control for SAB and PSFB converters.
  • Evaluate behaviour in both continuous and discontinuous conduction modes (CCM/DCM).
  • Compare several tuning approaches: manual tuning, transfer-function-based tuning and closed-loop Ziegler–Nichols.
  • Quantify sensitivity to disturbances in input current and output voltage for multiple converter designs.

My Contributions

  • Built averaged small-signal models of SAB and PSFB converters, including CICEA-based models for SAB/PSFB in DCM and a literature-based model for PSFB in CCM.
  • Derived analytical transfer functions and used them to compute PI gains from Bode plots and desired crossover frequencies.
  • Implemented manual and Ziegler–Nichols tuning loops directly in PLECS, including disturbance injection via lookup tables.
  • Ran extensive simulations on three different converter designs and documented settling time, overshoot and robustness for each topology and tuning method.

Methodology

  • Converters: SAB and PSFB rated for 5 kV ±10 % input, 20 kV ±10 % output and 20 MW, switching at 500 Hz.
  • Control law: PI controller regulating input voltage via duty-cycle / phase-shift modulation.
  • Disturbances: ±10 % steps on input current (renewable source) and output voltage (DC/AC stage) to emulate realistic grid events.
  • Tuning: comparison of transfer-function-based design vs. closed-loop Ziegler–Nichols and manual tuning for three sets of SAB/PSFB parameters in CCM and DCM.

Results & Insights

  • SAB is significantly more sensitive to input-current disturbances than to output-voltage variations; PSFB shows a more balanced response.
  • Ziegler–Nichols tuning consistently reduced input-voltage spikes compared to transfer-function-based tuning, often by more than half of the overshoot.
  • In DCM, SAB and PSFB exhibit very similar dynamics, confirming that the same tuning approach can be reused across topologies.
  • Carefully tuned manual PI controllers still achieved the best overall trade-off between speed and overshoot, underlining the value of engineering intuition on top of analytical tools.

Technologies

Power electronics modelling, SAB & PSFB topologies, DC/DC converter control, small-signal analysis, PI controller design, Ziegler–Nichols tuning, PLECS, Matlab/Simulink, medium-voltage DC systems.
