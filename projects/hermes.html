Hermes II Avionics Design

Semester project with the EPFL Rocket Team to redesign the avionics of the Hermes II supersonic rocket. I was responsible for the new electronics architecture, PCB design and validation of a compact, robust flight computer for high-altitude launches.

View full project report ↗

Overview

Hermes II is the second iteration of a solid-propellant, supersonic rocket developed by the EPFL Rocket Team. After earlier flights, the avionics were redesigned to improve reliability, data quality and integration inside the slim avionics bay. The new system had to acquire flight data, log it on-board, supervise the mission and withstand the mechanical and thermal constraints of supersonic flight.

My Contributions

  • Defined avionics architecture with a main board and three auxiliary sensor boards.
  • Selected key components (MCU, sensors, RF link, GPS and power electronics) balancing performance, size and availability.
  • Designed multi-layer PCBs, routing high-speed and sensitive analog signals under strict layout rules (e.g. GPS microstrip and ground plane constraints).
  • Planned and performed bench tests to validate power, communication buses and sensor behaviour before integration in the rocket.

Hardware Design

  • Main board: microcontroller, data logging, power management and interfaces to external boards in a compact four-layer PCB sized for the avionics bay.
  • Nosecone & static-port boards: pressure and temperature sensing, shaped to fit the nose and the static-pressure area of the structure.
  • Load-cell board: miniature circular PCB for thrust measurement and corresponding analog front-end.
  • Careful RF and GPS routing with controlled-impedance microstrip and placement rules to minimise interference and signal loss.

Key Learnings

  • Complete PCB workflow from requirements to manufactured hardware and bring-up.
  • Managing trade-offs between footprint, routing constraints and signal integrity.
  • Designing avionics that remain debuggable and testable despite tight mechanical limits.
